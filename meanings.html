Meaning of flowers

Daffodil

Daffodils bloom like a promise of new beginnings. They symbolize hope, rebirth, and the warmth of spring. In Greek mythology, the daffodil is linked to the tale of Narcissus, a boy who fell in love with his own reflection.

Ranunculus

Ranunculus flowers speak of radiant charm and sweet admiration. With their delicate, layered petals, they resemble a soft whisper of affection. They're often given to someone who brings light into your life.

Lily

Lilies represent purity, grace, and timeless beauty. In many cultures, they’re seen as symbols of motherhood and renewal. Ancient legends say lilies grew from the tears of goddesses.

Orchid

Orchids embody rare beauty, strength, and exotic elegance. They’ve long been symbols of refinement and deep emotional connection. In some legends, orchids are said to bloom where angels walk.

Poppy

Poppies carry a dreamy symbolism — of rest, remembrance, and imagination. Their bright petals often nod gently in the breeze, like soft lullabies. In myth, they were flowers of sleep and peace given by the gods.

Jasmine

Jasmine whispers of love, purity, and sweet nostalgia. Its fragrance fills the air like a gentle memory. In folk tales, jasmine is a flower of devotion, often worn by those waiting for a beloved.

Lotus

The lotus is a flower of resilience and spiritual awakening. Rising from muddy waters to bloom in beauty, it symbolizes inner peace and enlightenment. In Eastern traditions, it’s said to reflect the journey of the soul.

Peony

Peonies are lush with romance, honor, and gentle prosperity. Their full, soft blooms tell stories of heartfelt wishes and happy futures. In legend, the peony was a flower of healing, loved by ancient gods.

Hyacinth

Hyacinths speak of playful affection and sincere sorrow. Their scent is rich, almost nostalgic. In Greek myth, the flower bloomed from the blood of a beautiful youth, a gift from the god Apollo.

Lavender

Lavender brings calm, protection, and inner peace. Its soft purple hue and dreamy scent feel like a gentle hug. Once used to scent love letters, it’s said to carry messages straight to the heart.
